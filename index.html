EZ-grab

made in partnership with Duerstock IAS Labs at Purdue University

Challenge

Dr. Duerstock approached me with a problem at the beginning of my internship at the Duerstock IAS labs: he is quadraplegic and does not have any dexterity with his fingers, so grabbing small objects is a very hard task. He showed me a product pictured to the right, a grabber for small objects manually controlled by pushing a handle and therefore pushing the four prongs together. Becuase Dr. Duerstock could not use this device, he informed me that my project for the summer was to develop a product that could be used by individuals with disabilities and allow for easy grabbing and dropping of small objects.

role: Inividual Project, Project Lead
client: Dr. Brad Duerstock
skills:

  • CAD 3D Modelling
  • Physical Prototyping
  • Arduino Electronics
  • Circuit Design
  • Arduino Coding
  • 3D Printing
  • Design for 3D printing

benchmark testing with opensource 3D models

To start, I referenced the vast array of opensource 3D models on thingiverse.com. With a 3D printer readily available, I was able to print these models and observe their form and function.

Claw Machine Legs

This model was too big, and the mechanism was too clunky to be able to be shrunk down to a smaller size.

Telescoping Wand

This was a good and simple mechanism for manual telescoping, and was easy to print, and the shape could be easily altered.

Servo Actuated Robotic Arm

This model worked well for my intent as it was small, worked smoothly, and could be powered by a servo. Each iteration stemmed from this design.

design iteration 1: prevent from overrotation of gears

The first iteration involved designing a physical stopper for the servo rod. This would prevent the gears from overrotating or getting caught.

design iteration 2: attach claw to the telescoping rod

design iteration 3: moving the servo motor to the bottom of the handle

To move the servo motor to the bottom of the handle, it was important to design a mechanism that could translate rotation at the bottom of the device to the top where the claw grabber was located. I focused first only at the claw grabber, and after some research concluded that bevel gears would be good for this goal. These gears can translate rotation by 90 degrees, allowing for the servo motor to be in a 90 degree different direction from the gears of the claw grabber.


Next, it was important to be able to have this rotation work at any length of the rod. If top of the servo motor was attached with one rotating rod inside of the telescoping rod, the whole contraption would not be able to be telescoping. To fix this, I designed an inside telescoping rod that was able to rotate inside of the non-rotating outside telescoping rod. The hexagonal shape of the inside rotation rod was developed to ensure minimal slippage while rotating.

design iteration 4: updated attachment of the claw grabber to the telescoping rod

To attach the newly designed claw grabber to the telescoping rod, I extended the attachment at a height that was just above the vertical bevel gear to allow it to sit comfortably and be attached by super glue.

3D CAD rendering and animation

Here is the final CAD animation of the EZ Grab. The 3D model was made in AutoCAD Fusion 360 and brought into Blender to be rendered and animated.

Slingshot

swift. safe. smart

Challenge

In my Industrial Design Class, we were tasked with a project: the Future Hobby Project. We were asked three simple questions:

What is the Hobby? Why do you like it? How has it impacted your life?

These questions served as a guide to a challenge of creating a product that can contribute to the future of my selected Hobby. As shown to the right, my hobby is photography, and is something that I enjoy doing during my down-time.

The challenge of this project was to create a product that can contribute to the field of our selected hobby, and solve a problem that is happening now and will happen in the future.

role: Inividual Project, Project Lead
class: Industrial Design Projects
skills:

  • CAD 3D Modelling
  • Keyshot Rendering
  • Visual Brand Language
  • Concept Design

brainstorming with a mind map

I started with the idea of Photography, and branched off ideation from the center to create as many categories as possible for brainstorming. This helped give a thorough overview of the activity and helped to organize the many different complexities of the activity.

Secondary Research into camera gear and its future

Next I looked into secondary research about cameras to help guide my brainstorming and look into areas that can be improved upon.
